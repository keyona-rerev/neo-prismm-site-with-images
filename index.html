The challenge

Banks know how to open accounts. They have no system for when customers die.

Every year, financial institutions lose billions in deposits to estate attrition, not because customers chose to leave, but because there is no infrastructure to manage the transition of accounts at death.

The result: assets leave to competitors, attorneys, and friction. Beneficiaries are lost. Compliance risk compounds. Operations teams spend months per case on manual, paper-driven processes.
